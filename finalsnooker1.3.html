Snooker & Pool Rules

Understanding the fundamental rules of snooker and pool

Snooker Gameplay

  • Snooker is played on a rectangular table covered with green cloth
  • The game is played with 21 object balls: 15 reds and 6 colored balls
  • Players use a cue to strike the white cue ball
  • The objective is to score more points than the opponent
  • Points are scored by potting balls in the correct sequence

Snooker Scoring

  • Red balls are worth 1 point each
  • Yellow ball: 2 points
  • Green ball: 3 points
  • Brown ball: 4 points
  • Blue ball: 5 points
  • Pink ball: 6 points
  • Black ball: 7 points

Snooker Sequence

  • Players must alternate between potting red and colored balls
  • After potting a red, the player must nominate a color
  • Colored balls are returned to the table after being potted
  • When only colored balls remain, they are potted in ascending order
  • The player with the most points at the end wins

Snooker Fouls

  • Failing to hit the correct ball first
  • Potting the cue ball
  • Hitting a ball off the table
  • Touching any ball with anything other than the cue
  • Foul penalties award points to the opponent (minimum 4 points)

Pool (8-Ball) Rules

  • Pool is played with 15 object balls and a cue ball
  • Balls are divided into solids (1-7) and stripes (9-15)
  • The 8-ball is black and must be potted last
  • Players must pocket all their group before attempting the 8-ball
  • The first player to legally pocket the 8-ball wins

Etiquette

  • Remain seated while your opponent is playing
  • Avoid making noise or distractions
  • Shake hands before and after the match
  • Accept the referee's decisions with grace
  • Respect the equipment and venue at all times
